APJ Abdul Kalam - The Missile Man of India and 11th President of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and studied aerospace engineering.

Kalam was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He played a crucial organizational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.

After his presidency, he continued to be a visiting professor, writing, and inspiring millions through his books, speeches, and interactions with students and public alike. Kalam's dedication to education and his vision for a developed India made him a beloved figure.

Education and Early Life

Born in a humble family, Kalam's early life was marked by hardship and perseverance. He studied physics and aerospace engineering and graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960.

Career Highlights

Dr. Kalam's career spanned four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

  • Project Director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP).
  • Played a pivotal role in developing the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Instrumental in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests, making India a nuclear-armed state.

Presidency (2002-2007)

During his tenure as the President of India, Kalam was affectionately known as the "People's President". He was committed to interacting with the youth and encouraging them to dream big.
